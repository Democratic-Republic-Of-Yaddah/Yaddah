Itay Dayan

Itay Dayan (born 10 June 2010) is a Yaddahan Micronationalist, the founder of the Democratic Republic of Yaddah and currently serves as its Prime Minister.

Biography

Itay Dayan was born on June 10, 2010, at the Sheba Medical Center in Ramat Gan to Hagar, a former executive at Bank Jerusalem and entrepreneur, and to Ilan, a manager at Elbit Systems. At a young age, he moved with his family to live in the heart of Gedera. He was educated at the Farg Kindergarten, the "HaBiluyim" elementary school, and the "Darka Begin" high school in a science-oriented class. In 9th grade, he transferred to the "Atid" High School for Science in Lod, which is part of the "Atid" educational network. In early 2024, Dayan began working on establishing a micronation. During this time, he developed national symbols such as a flag, emblem, a system of government, and even a political ideology by which the country would operate.

Beliefs

Itay Dayan, the founder of Yaddahism, has shaped his political ideology around a vision that seeks to balance modern liberalism, Zionism, and capitalism, while maintaining state control over key public institutions. His beliefs emphasize a strong, transparent government that plays an active role in regulating the economy and protecting national security. Dayan views Zionism as an essential unifying force for the Jewish people, but he rejects racist or exclusionary views, advocating for a society where citizens, regardless of ethnicity, are treated equally under the law. Dayan is a firm believer in the importance of personal freedoms and equality of rights, which are core principles of modern liberalism. However, he also stresses the need for a strong state apparatus to safeguard the welfare of the population and ensure the stability of society. This balance, according to Dayan, is key to creating a society where both individual rights and collective responsibilities are respected. In terms of economic policy, Dayan supports a capitalist system that encourages private entrepreneurship and market competition. Nevertheless, he advocates for partial regulation to prevent exploitation, ensure economic fairness, and guarantee access to essential goods and services. He believes this approach is necessary to protect citizens from the instability that can arise in a completely free-market economy. Regarding the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, Dayan opposes the two-state solution, advocating instead for a more assertive approach to ensuring Israel’s security. He believes that Israel must maintain control over strategic areas and take aggressive actions to advance its national interests. While his stance is controversial, Dayan argues that this approach is the most effective way to achieve long-term peace and security for the Jewish state. Through these beliefs, Dayan seeks to establish a political system that upholds democratic values, including respect for human rights, the rule of law, and governmental transparency. He views transparency as essential in building trust with the public and preventing corruption within the political system. Ultimately, Dayan’s vision for Yaddahism is one that prioritizes national unity, social equity, and economic prosperity, all while safeguarding Israel's security and sovereignty.
